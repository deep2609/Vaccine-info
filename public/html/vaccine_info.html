COVAXIN

About the vaccine
  • COVAXIN, India's indigenous COVID-19 vaccine by Bharat Biotech is developed in collaboration with the Indian Council of Medical Research (ICMR) - National Institute of Virology (NIV).
  • Covaxin is an inactivated vaccine which means that it is made up of killed coronaviruses, making it safe to be injected into the body.
  • The two doses are given four weeks apart. The vaccine can be stored at 2°C to 8°C.

Effectiveness

  • Phase 3 results of the COVAXIN, developed by Indian Council of Medical Research (ICMR) in partnership with Bharat BiotechInternational Limited (BBIL),has shown an interim vaccine efficacy of 81% in preventing Covid-19.
  • In an ongoing clinical trial, COVAXIN has been shown to generate immunity following 2 doses given 4 weeks apart.
  • COVAXIN’s ability to neutralize UK variant strain of SARS-CoV-2 has also recently been established..

Pricing

  • On April 29, Bharat Biotech announced a cut in the price of 'Covaxin' for states to Rs 400 per dose from the earlier Rs 600 per dose.
  • The company is supplying vaccines to Andhra Pradesh, Assam, Chhattisgarh, Gujarat, Jammu & Kashmir, Jharkhand, Madhya Pradesh, Odisha, Tamil Nadu, Telangana, Uttar Pradesh and West Bengal.

COVISHIELD

About the vaccine
  • The Oxford–AstraZeneca COVID-19 vaccine, and sold under the brand names Covishield and Vaxzevria
  • Developed by Oxford University and AstraZeneca, it is given by intramuscular injection, using as a vector the modified chimpanzee adenovirus ChAdOx1.
  • The vaccine has a good safety profile, with side effects including injection-site pain, headache, and nausea, all generally resolving within a few days

Effectiveness

  • Based on data provided by the manufacturer, the Astrazeneca-Oxford vaccine, or AZD1222, has shown to be 63% effective in an ongoing, large-scale clinical trial.
  • In ongoing clinical trials, the COVISHIELD Vaccine has been shown to prevent COVID-19 disease following 2 doses given between 4 and 12 weeks apart.
  • You may get protective immune response 4 weeks after the second dose of COVISHIELD vaccine.

Pricing

  • From May 1, Serum Institute of India priced its vaccine Covishield at Rs 400 per dose for the central and state governments and Rs 600 per dose for private hospitals.
  • According to Times of India, the Covishield manufactured by the Serum Institute of India ranges from Rs 700-900 per Dose at Private Hospitals
